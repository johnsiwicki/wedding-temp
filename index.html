FAQS

When is the wedding?

The civil ceremony will begin promptly at 11 am on Sunday November 8th, 2015.

The cocktail hour will begin at half 11:30 am, followed by a brunch reception at 12:30 in the afternoon.

How do I get to the venue?

Please visit this page from the New Haven Museum’s website for directions.

Please consult the customised map (link) for walking directions, and the locations of additional parking and hotels.

What time should I arrive?

The ceremony will begin promptly at 11 o’clock in the morning, and will be on the shorter side, so it may work best to arrive about fifteen minutes before 11.

Remember that New Haven has many one-way streets and extremely limited free parking, so it is wise to give yourself plenty of time.

Where should I park?

There is limited free parking in the back of the museum. Additionally, there is free parking in a Yale-owned lot just around the corner from the museum on Trumbull Street.

Please consult this page from the New Haven Museum’s website for more information.

Will I have to pay for parking?

Parking in New Haven: There is limited metered street parking in New Haven, and the Omni, Study and New Haven hotels will have garage parking for a fee. In addition to using change or a credit card to pay for street parking, you can download the Parkmobile App to your mobile device and use that instead.

Parking for the wedding: Yale University opens most of its many parking lots to the public on the weekends. Lot 37 on Trumbull Street near the museum is one of these, so paying to park on the day of reception should not be necessary.

What should I wear?

Comfortable, semi formal attire is suggested: jackets or suits; short or long cocktail or formal dresses.

Is there a dress code?

No.

I am coming from out of town. Where should I stay?

The three main hotels in town are the Omni Hotel on Temple Street and Church Street, the New Haven Hotel on George Street and Temple Street, and the Study Hotel on Chapel Street and Park Street.

Please consult Travel & Accommodations section for more details.

When is the RSVP deadline?

The favor of a reply is requested by the eighth of October, 2015.

Are the ceremony and reception locations wheelchair accessible?

Yes, please contact the New Haven Museum for further details.

What should/could I do between the ceremony and the reception?

1) Eat, drink and be merry! 2) Explore the different exhibitions on view at the New Haven Museum!

The cocktail hour and reception will be in the same location (though a different floor of the building) as the ceremony.

Will food be served?

Yes, hors d’oeuvres and brunch will served. Eat a light breakfast!

What kind of food will be served?

Many, if not most, menu options will be dairy free, gluten free and/or vegetarian, though there will be meat/dairy options available as well. No seafood or shellfish will be present, as the groom has a severe allergy.

What if I have a dietary restriction?

We have taken care to make the menu as flexible as possible for those with allergies or restrictions. If you have further dietary concerns, please let us know when you RSVP. The caterer is very flexible and can manage many requests.

Will there be dancing?

Yes!

Can I take pictures?

We have hired a professional photographer and have entrusted her with the sole responsibility of capturing the special moments of the day. Guests are welcome to take pictures during the reception, but we do respectfully ask that guests refrain from taking pictures during the ceremony - this includes pictures taken with mobile devices.

We thank you most sincerely for your understanding!

What time will the reception end?

The reception should end closer to 4 o’clock in the afternoon.

Is there transportation being provided between reception and hotels?

There will not be transportation provided between the New Haven Museum and hotels in town, however the museum is about a 20 minute walk from the Study Hotel, and a 15 to 17 minute walk from the New Haven and Omni Hotels. Via car, the travel time drops to about ten minutes or less.

Please refer to the list of car services/cabs in the Travel & Accommodations section for more details.

Is there a gifts registry?

We would like nothing more than the pleasure of your company to help us celebrate our special day. Please refer to the Registry section of our web site for more information.

Will last names be changing after the wedding?

As indicated in the at-home cards sent with guests’ invitations, the bride and groom will remain John Siwicki and Jennifer Nolte after their wedding.

Please feel free to email John (johnsiwicki AT gmail dot com) or Jenn (retrojayne AT gmail dot com) with any questions or concerns.
